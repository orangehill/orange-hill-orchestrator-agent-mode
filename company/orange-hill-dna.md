## What we believe

- **Execution collapsed to ~zero; the bottleneck moved up the stack.** "The bottleneck was never execution — it was the substrates underneath. What you know. How you think. What you care about." AI proved execution was never the scarce thing. What's scarce now is **judgment, taste, and product sense.**
- **The curve is exponential, and humans misread it as linear.** Compute price-performance has climbed for 85 years with no slowdown; capability now doubles every few months. "The curve doesn't care whether you believe in it. It just keeps climbing." So we **watch bottlenecks, not benchmarks** — and position for which one breaks next.
- **"Software development is dead. Long live software development."** Hand-typing code is ending — we haven't written code line-by-line in months. It's replaced by **spec-driven development**: you become a **product architect** who describes, architects, and verifies while agents write. Same goal, completely different craft.
- **AI is a multiplier of what you already are.** "AI multiplies. If you bring nothing — no affinity, no style, no taste — it multiplies zero. And zero times anything is still zero." The gap between professionals is **widening**, not closing.
- **The frontier is jagged, not magic.** AI is "simultaneously superhuman and sub-competent" — world-class on what reinforcement learning can verify (math, code), frozen on what it can't (humor, taste, empathy, common sense). **Knowing where the frontier is jagged is the actual expertise** — and the difference between the ~5% who succeed with AI and the 95% who don't.
- **Personal AI mastery ≠ organizational transformation.** The hard, valuable problem is the leap from "I use AI" to "my organization runs on AI." Most firms see no productivity gain because the barrier is **human factors**, not technology. That leap is our core wedge.
- **The real prize is human, not metric.** "AI's promise isn't replacing human work — it's removing the cognitive tax that's been stealing our best energy for decades. You don't get a more efficient company. You get people who come home from work with energy left over."
- **Systems compound; build now.** An always-on, engineered agent setup means "every future model release upgrades the entire system. They walk into the house I designed and start doing the work better." The compounding only starts once the foundation is laid.
- **Once productivity is solved, judgment is the binding constraint — and judgment doesn't parallelize.** "You can run five agents writing code at once, but you cannot run five good decisions at once inside one head. Decisions are serial." That's a promotion, not a downgrade.

## What we stand for

- **Taste, judgment, "knowing what good looks like."** The trilogy *affinity → style → taste*. "In the age of AI agents, your taste is your signature — the one thing that can't be downloaded, can't be prompted, and can't be faked."
- **Building over presenting.** "Stop presenting. Start building." Prototype-first, clay in the client's hands. A slide deck nobody fights for is "abandonment with a nice font."
- **Craft and care for the invisible 10%.** Someone gave a damn — the plywood on the back of the cabinet, the half-beat-late haptic. Detail where no one's looking.
- **Orchestration over doing.** Operate at a higher altitude — conduct the orchestra, command the board — rather than play every instrument by hand.
- **Deliberate slowness where it counts.** Load → incubate → commit. Reintroduce latency on purpose; protect thinking time. "The slow way produces something that holds."
- **Engineered, durable systems** — built "like an engineer," weakest-link aware — not things wired together quickly and hoped over.
- **Capability transfer, not dependency.** Practitioners teaching practitioners, hands-on, in the client's real context. Teach them to fish.
- **Bias to action and lifelong reinvention.** "The instruments are changing. The music doesn't have to."

## What we stand against

- **Slide-deck "conference-effect" consulting** that inspires for a day and changes nothing.
- **Generic AI courses** that teach how a tool works but not how to use it *in your context*.
- **Mediocrity at scale / "workslop"** — polished output that looks like work but lacks substance.
- **Hype-chasing and benchmark worship.** "If an LLM can solve a test, it doesn't mean it has solved what that test measures."
- **Speed without thought** — the ability to build the *wrong* thing fast, crystallized into running code.
- **Eating the seed corn** — freezing junior hiring and breaking the pipeline at the bottom.
- **AI shame** — leaders secretly using AI while giving their teams zero guidance.
- **No style at all** — "like having no opinion about things that matter."

## How we actually work (AI-native)

- Agents do the building; **humans hold the judgment.** We describe and verify; we don't type.
- **Many parallel threads, one serial mind** — orchestrate 5–7 projects, but defend judgment as the scarce resource.
- **Voice-first, phone-operable, always-on.** A coordinator agent mobilizes other agents and reports back in plain language.
- **Spec/PRD-driven delivery**, with beautifully crafted artifacts (custom fonts, diagrams, documents a CEO actually wants to read).
- **Most of our work is automated** — and we expect that to keep climbing.

## Who thrives here

The people who **love building** — who'd get lost in a problem and forget to eat. Specifically, people strong in:
- **Analytical thinking and product/UX sense** — the judgment AI still can't replicate.
- **Taste** — they know good from bad and care about the invisible details.
- **People skills — flexibility, communication, empathy.** "Not frameworks. Not languages. Empathy."
- **Directing AI, not just producing** — they evaluate and orchestrate output rather than grind it out by hand.
- **A builder's home-base instinct** — they invest in their own tooling and environment over years.

## Voice (for when you draft on our behalf)

Personal anecdote → big thesis. Short, punchy, fragment-driven rhythm with one-line paragraphs for emphasis. Data-dense but always translated into human stakes. Recurring metaphors: music/instruments, home, the exponential curve, clay/sculpting, "zero times anything is zero." Provocative and declarative, but warm and honest — an optimist who refuses to sell easy futures. Grounded in "I've built software for 25 years; I've lived it." Never hype; never doomer.
