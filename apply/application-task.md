# The application task

One task. It's deliberately the actual job in miniature, and you're encouraged to **use your AI to do it** — that's the point.

## Send us two things

**1. One thing you've shipped that you're proud of — and what *you* specifically decided.**
Not a CV dump. A short story of one project, product, or system you built or led, and the judgment calls that were yours: what you chose to do, what you chose *not* to do, and why.

**2. How you'd run a real Orange Hill week.**
We've written up an anonymized but real snapshot of three of our projects running at once — one of them on fire — in **[`the-week.md`](the-week.md)**. Read it, then write your one-page plan for the week:
- what you'd do yourself vs. where you'd point AI agents (and which agents, at what);
- what you protect and finish on the project with the immovable date — and what you'd let degrade gracefully;
- what you'd let wait, hand off, or escalate on the other two;
- how you'd keep all three from silently slipping (your check-ins, your "done enough" for the week).

We're reading **#2** for how you *organize, prioritize, and sequence* — not for the "right" answer. There isn't one. Show us the shape of your thinking.

## Using your agent (encouraged)
Ask the agent you connected to this repo:
> "Help me do Orange Hill's application task for the orchestrator role. Use what you know about me for #1, and let's design my parallel-projects plan for #2. Push me where it's vague."

If your agent helped — good. That's how we work too. We care that the thinking and the judgment are yours.

## How to send it
Apply at **https://orangehill.ai/en/careers/orchestrator** with both parts — a doc, a repo, a Loom, whatever shows you best. **We reply to everyone who does this task.**
