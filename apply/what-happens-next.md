# What happens next (our process, honestly)

We keep it short and respectful of your time. We're a small team hiring deliberately — we'd rather move carefully and get the fit right than run a 7-round gauntlet.

## The steps
1. **You do the application task** ([`application-task.md`](application-task.md)). Everyone who does it gets a reply — that's a promise.
2. **A quick exchange** — a short written back-and-forth or a brief call to make sure there's mutual interest.
3. **A real conversation** with the team — not a trivia quiz. We talk about how you think, how you work, and what you'd own.
4. **A short, hands-on task** — role-relevant and deliberately small (think an hour or two, not a take-home that eats your weekend). It's a real slice of the work, not spec work we'd keep — if you know your craft, you'll move through it fast. We keep it short out of respect for your time.
5. **References / work samples** — we like to see real things you've built and hear from people you've worked with.
6. **Offer.**

## How we think about comp
Base + meaningful performance and ownership upside. We discuss real ranges **early** so nobody wastes time, and we don't lowball senior people — the whole point of this role is leverage, and we pay for it. Details in [`faq.md`](faq.md).

## Honesty both ways
- We'll tell you plainly if we don't think it's a fit, and why.
- We'd like the same from you. If your agent told you this isn't your role, trust it — no hard feelings.
- Timing: we're hiring now, ahead of a planned move to Austria later this year. We're happy to work around notice periods and life logistics.

Questions we didn't answer? Ask your agent (it has [`faq.md`](../faq.md)), or just ask us.
