# FAQ (the honest answers)

## "Is this really not traditional coding?"
Correct. You'll architect, judge, and orchestrate — agents do the typing. If your love is being heads-down in the code yourself, you'd miss it here. If your love is *shipping the right thing* and you're happy directing, this is leverage you can't get as a solo IC.

## "Why Orange Hill?"
Real autonomy and ownership, a front-row seat building our own AI-first products (not just client work), a small senior team to think with, work at the frontier of AI-native delivery, and an EU/Austria path as we expand. High leverage, low bureaucracy, real upside. If that's what you're after, we're hard to beat.

## "What's the comp?"
Base + meaningful performance and ownership upside. We don't publish a number here because we set it against the specific person and discuss real ranges early. We don't lowball this role — it's a high-leverage seat and we pay accordingly.

## "Remote? Austria? Relocation?"
We're Serbia-based and expanding into Austria / DACH. We're flexible on working arrangement and will be straight with you about what the role needs and where it's heading. Life logistics and notice periods are workable.

## "Will AI just replace this role in two years?"
We think the opposite — read `company/orange-hill-dna.md`. As execution gets cheaper, the scarce thing becomes judgment, taste, and orchestration: exactly this role. The work moves *up* the stack, not away.

## "How big is the team? Who would I work with?"
Small and senior — a few high-leverage people plus a lot of agents. You'd own delivery across multiple projects with real autonomy, working directly with the founder.

## "What if your agent tells me I'm not a fit?"
Then it probably saved us both time — and you should trust it. We built it to be honest, not to recruit you. No hard feelings, and feel free to point a strong friend our way.

## "Is this whole agent-mode thing a gimmick?"
It's a filter and a flex. We've handed most of the building to AI; a job post you can *talk to* is just us being ourselves. If you found it delightful, you're probably our kind of person. If you found it gimmicky, that's useful too.
